# Blog Post Templates for SEO Content

---

## Template 1: The Definitive Guide (Informational Intent)

Best for: "How to...", "What is...", "Guide to...", "Everything you need to know about..."

```
TITLE: [Specific Topic]: The Complete Guide for [Audience] in [Year]
Example: Planning Your First Egypt Trip: The Complete Guide for Americans in 2025

STRUCTURE:
H1: [Same as title]

[Lead paragraph: Why this matters to the reader. What they'll know by the end.]

## Quick Summary / TL;DR
[3–5 bullet points of the key takeaways for skimmers]

## [Main Topic Section 1]
[Subheading with specific angle]
[Content with first-hand details, specific examples]

## [Main Topic Section 2]
[Continue building the complete picture]

## Common Questions About [Topic]
[FAQ section — use FAQPage schema]
- Q: [Question]
  A: [Specific, direct answer]

## Final Thoughts / What to Do Next
[Summarize + CTA]
```

## Template 2: The Safety/Concern Post (American Travel Audience)

Best for: "Is [destination] safe?", "What should I know before visiting?"

```
TITLE: Is [Destination] Safe for American Tourists in [Year]? An Honest Assessment

H1: Is [Destination] Safe for American Tourists in [Year]?

[Opening: Direct answer first — don't bury the lead. "Yes, and here's the context."]

## What the US State Department Says
[Current advisory level + what it actually means]

## The Reality on the Ground
[First-hand perspective — what visitors actually experience]
[Specific areas that are safe vs. areas to avoid]

## Common Safety Concerns (Addressed)
### [Concern 1, e.g., "Petty Theft"]
[Direct, honest answer]

### [Concern 2, e.g., "Political Stability"]
[Direct, honest answer]

### [Concern 3, e.g., "Health and Medical"]
[Direct, honest answer]

## Practical Safety Tips
[Specific, actionable advice — not vague platitudes]

## Frequently Asked Questions
[FAQ schema opportunity]

## Bottom Line
[Clear conclusion + what you offer as a tour operator to address safety]
[CTA: "Our guides are locals who know exactly where to go and what to avoid..."]
```

## Template 3: The Itinerary Post (Commercial Intent)

Best for: "[X] days in Egypt", "Egypt itinerary", "what to do in Egypt"

```
TITLE: [X]-Day Egypt Itinerary: The Perfect [Type] Trip for [Audience]
Example: 10-Day Egypt Itinerary: The Perfect First-Timer's Trip for American Travelers

H1: [Same as title]

[Intro: Who this itinerary is for, what makes it special, quick overview]

## Is This Itinerary Right for You?
[Short qualifier — budget range, pace, physical requirements]

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1: [Location] — [Main Activity]
[What you do, why it matters, what to expect, tips]
[Accommodation: [type]]

### Day 2: [Location] — [Main Activity]
[Continue...]

[...all days...]

## What's Included vs. What You Arrange Yourself
[Clear breakdown — sets expectations]

## Customizing This Itinerary
[How to adjust for different group sizes, budgets, interests]

## Book This Trip
[Direct CTA with pricing info]
[Contact details]
```

## Template 4: The Comparison Post (Commercial Research Intent)

Best for: "best egypt tour operators", "X vs Y", "private vs group tours egypt"

```
TITLE: [Option A] vs [Option B]: Which Is Right for Your Egypt Trip?
Example: Private Egypt Tours vs. Group Tours: How to Choose (From People Who Run Both)

H1: [Same as title]

[Intro: Frame the decision — there's no universal right answer, it depends on these factors]

## At a Glance: Quick Comparison Table
| Factor | Private Tour | Group Tour |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Cost | Higher | Lower |
| Flexibility | High | Fixed |
| ... | ... | ... |

## [Option A]: Who It's For
[Describe ideal candidate, benefits, drawbacks]
[Be honest — don't oversell]

## [Option B]: Who It's For
[Describe ideal candidate, benefits, drawbacks]

## The Factors That Should Drive Your Decision
### Budget
### Group size
### How much you want flexibility
### Travel style

## Our Recommendation
[Take a position — don't be wishy-washy. Credibility comes from having a clear POV]

## Frequently Asked Questions
[FAQ schema]

## Next Step
[CTA based on which option the reader leans toward]
```
